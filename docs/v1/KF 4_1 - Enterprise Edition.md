Knowledge Factory (KF)
KF 4_1 — Enterprise Edition
Version 1.0 (Architecture Freeze)
Chapter 1 — Introduction

The Enterprise Edition defines the architectural capabilities required to deploy the Knowledge Factory Platform in large-scale, security-sensitive, and highly regulated environments.

It extends the core platform with enterprise-grade governance, security, scalability, resilience, compliance, and operational management while remaining fully compatible with the standard Knowledge Factory architecture.

Enterprise Edition is an operational deployment model, not a separate architectural platform.

Chapter 2 — Purpose

The Enterprise Edition enables organisations to:

Deploy Knowledge Factory at enterprise scale.

Support multiple business units.

Protect sensitive knowledge assets.

Meet regulatory obligations.

Integrate with enterprise infrastructure.

Ensure business continuity.

Operate in cloud, hybrid, or isolated environments.

Manage long-term organisational knowledge.

Chapter 3 — Enterprise Principles

The Enterprise Edition follows ten principles.

One platform architecture.

Zero-trust security model.

Operational resilience.

Observability across the platform.

Long-term maintainability.

Chapter 15 — Architectural Impact

The Enterprise Edition extends the Knowledge Factory Platform into enterprise and mission-critical environments without introducing a separate architecture.

By adding enterprise-grade security, governance, scalability, resilience, compliance, operational management, and deployment flexibility, organisations can confidently adopt the platform for long-term management of strategic professional knowledge.

The Enterprise Edition therefore completes the Knowledge Factory Version 1 architecture, demonstrating that the same foundational principles supporting individual knowledge engineers can also support global enterprises, governments, research institutions, and regulated industries while preserving a single, coherent architectural vision.
