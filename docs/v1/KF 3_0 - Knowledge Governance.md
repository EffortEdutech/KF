Chapter 1 — Introduction

Knowledge Governance establishes the policies, principles, processes, and controls that ensure every Knowledge Object within the Knowledge Factory Platform is trustworthy, traceable, accountable, and professionally governed.

While the Knowledge Manufacturing Pipeline defines how knowledge is produced, Knowledge Governance defines how knowledge earns and maintains trust throughout its lifecycle.

Knowledge Governance applies to every Knowledge Object, Mission, Workforce Member, and Professional Knowledge Asset within the platform.

Chapter 2 — Purpose

The objectives of Knowledge Governance are to:

Establish trust.

Protect knowledge integrity.

Ensure professional quality.

Maintain complete provenance.

Support regulatory compliance.

Enable transparent decision-making.

Preserve institutional knowledge.

Govern lifecycle evolution.

Protect intellectual property.

Build confidence in Professional Knowledge Assets.

Trust is considered a measurable architectural property rather than a subjective opinion.

Chapter 3 — Governance Principles

Knowledge Factory adopts ten governing principles.

Every Knowledge Object must have an owner.

Every published object must be traceable to its sources.

Every significant decision must be auditable.

AI assists but never becomes the final authority.

Human accountability is mandatory for publication.

Governance is continuous throughout the object lifecycle.

Quality is measurable.

Intellectual property is protected.

Policies are consistently enforced.

Governance evolves with knowledge.

Governance policies define the rules that control knowledge creation, modification, validation, publication, and retirement.

Policies are version-controlled Knowledge Objects.

Chapter 6 — Provenance Framework

Every Knowledge Object shall maintain complete provenance.

Provenance includes:

Original source references.

Acquisition history.

AI generation history.

Human modifications.

Validation records.

Publication history.

Digital signatures.

Previous versions.

Every published Knowledge Object must be explainable from origin to current version.

Validation ensures technical correctness before publication.

Validation consists of multiple layers.

Examples include:

Duplicate detection.

Structural consistency.

Metadata completeness.

Rule compliance.

Relationship integrity.

Knowledge Engineering Review

Examples include:

Ontology consistency.

Classification quality.

Object composition.

Metadata quality.

Subject Matter Expert Review

Technical accuracy.

Industry relevance.

Professional judgement.

Standards compliance.

Only successfully validated Knowledge Objects may proceed to publication.

Chapter 15 — Architectural Impact

Knowledge Governance establishes the trust architecture of the Knowledge Factory Platform.

It ensures that every Knowledge Object is created, reviewed, validated, published, maintained, and retired according to transparent and measurable governance processes.

By integrating provenance, validation, trust assessment, compliance, intellectual property protection, lifecycle management, and certification into a unified governance framework, the platform transforms AI-generated information into professionally governed knowledge.

Knowledge Governance therefore represents the foundation of confidence upon which all Professional Knowledge Assets are built, enabling organisations to rely on the Knowledge Factory as a trusted source of professional intelligence rather than simply another AI system.

Architectural Reflection

Completing KF 3_0 marks another major milestone.

The platform now has three complete architectural layers:

Foundation (KF 1_x) — Defines the philosophy, core concepts, manufacturing process, and workforce.

Core Platform (KF 2_x) — Defines the runtime architecture and operational services.

Knowledge Governance (KF 3_0) — Defines how trust, quality, accountability, and intellectual property are established.

This means the next document, KF 3_1 — Professional Knowledge Assets, can focus entirely on the commercial products manufactured by the platform. Rather than discussing architecture, it will define the structure, packaging, versioning, composition, and lifecycle of the products that customers ultimately purchase and use—such as Professional Knowledge Packs, ontologies, workflow libraries, rule libraries, and certified domain intelligence. At that point, the Knowledge Factory story will naturally transition from building the factory to shipping its products.
